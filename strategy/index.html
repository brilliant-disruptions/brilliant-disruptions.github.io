The Assembly Line

Build and ship products in a continuous relay so all three of us stay unblocked. Every version is sellable. The line never stops, and the team is never idle.

01 · The Crew

Three operators, one relay

Clear lanes with deliberate overlap — so a handoff is never a cold start.

J
JimmyCOO Business-minded with real technical capability. Significant overlap with Michael — the two of them are the build engine.
M
MichaelCTO Technical lead with business acumen. Owns the architecture and keeps the build moving from v1 to v2 and beyond.
N
NickCMO Less technical, strong on branding and marketing. Takes a finished v1 and gets it into customers' hands.
02 · Core Strategy

The line, step by step

A continuous relay. By the time customers are happy with v1, v2 is already on the truck.

1

Build v1

Michael + Jimmy ship a fully shippable, sellable product — not a half-baked MVP.

Michael · Jimmy
2

Hand to market

Nick takes v1 and runs all marketing to get it into customers' hands.

Nick
3

Build v2

Michael · Jimmy
4

Fold in feedback

Customer feedback flows into v2 — new features ready the moment v1 lands.

Full crew
03 · The Priority Rule

The main product always comes first

  • →Ship v1 — Nick starts marketing immediately.
  • →Keep v2 as the primary build effort.
  • →If the team is ever waiting — on feedback or marketing — spin up another product rather than sitting idle.
  • →The main product never loses priority to the side work.
Key Principle · Non-negotiable

Every version is shippable. v1 is a sellable product — not a prototype with known gaps Nick has to apologize for.

04 · Holes to Watch

Where the line can jam

Speed only holds if we keep these from stalling the queue.

Feedback loop blocking

If Nick's marketing needs iteration from the technical side, the build queue can stall.

Messy handoffs

Nick will have product questions and feature requests that pull Michael + Jimmy back in.

Hidden coupling

Shared infrastructure — auth, backend, troubleshooting time — turns parallel builds into friction.

05 · Open Question

Which product takes the first lap?

The model is set. The only thing left to decide is what goes on the line first — and that decision starts the clock on week one.
